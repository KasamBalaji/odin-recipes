Sambar

..Mouth Savoring Lentil Soup from India


Ingredients

  • 2 cups Tuvar dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 teaspoon Haldi (Turmeric powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup Chopped Lauki (bottle gourd)
  • 2 small Small eggplant/baingan
  • 1/2 cup Chopped Zucchini
  • 1/2 cup Chopped Carrots
  • 1 medium Chopped tomato
  • 2 tablespoon Gur or Jaggery
  • 3 tablespoon Tamarind juice
  • 1 large onion chopped
  • 2-3 tablespoon Oil to cook veggies
  • 2 tablespoon Homemade Sambar powder
  • 2 cups of Water added to sambar
  • 1 teaspoon Mustard seeds
  • 3/4 teaspoon Methi dana (Fenugreek seeds)
  • 2 teaspoon Skinned and split urad dal
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin seed
  • A pinch of Hing (asofetadia)
  • 10 pieces of Curry leaves
  • 4 pieces of Whole Red chillies
  • 2 tablespoon Oil

Steps for Preparation

  1. Assemble ingredients to boil dal.
  2. Clean and wash dal.
  3. Transfer dal to a pressure cooker, add salt and turmeric powder. Cover and pressure cook for 2-3 whistles.
  4. Allow the steam to escape before opening the lid, dal all done and cooked.
  5. Whisk the dal. Set it aside.
  6. Soak tamarind in warm water for 15-20 minutes. Use a spoon to mash up the lumps. Strain and set it aside.
  7. Wash and assemble veggies for dal. Chop the veggies into cubes.
  8. Heat oil in a pan, add carrots and cook for 1 minute.
  9. Toss in onion, baingan/small eggplant, tomato, zucchini and lauki.
  10. Cover and cook for 2-3 minutes or until veggies are fork tender. Add jaggery sambar powder, and mix it well.
  11. Add veggies to the cooked dal.
  12. Add tamarind water to the cooked dal. Mix until well blended. Cover and cook for 4-5 minutes on a medium-low flame.
  13. Assemble ingredients for tempering..
  14. Heat oil in a pan. Add all the ingredients one by one.
  15. As they start to crackle, add curry leaves..
  16. And pour this tadka to the boiling dal.
  17. Mix and allow it to simmer for another 5-10 minutes.
  18. Adjust seasonings and..
  19. Serve hot with Warm instant rice idlis.
